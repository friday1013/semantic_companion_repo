## The Tamarian Insight

In "Darmok" (ST:TNG S5E2), the Children of Tama communicate exclusively
through narrative reference — shared stories as the atomic unit of meaning.
"Darmok and Jalad at Tanagra" does not *describe* cooperation. It *is*
cooperation, instantiated in a story both speakers carry. The meaning is
inseparable from the narrative vessel. You cannot extract the proposition
without destroying what made it true.

Dathon brought Picard to El-Adrel to *create a shared story* — because
without that, no communication was possible. The shared experience is the
prerequisite for shared meaning.

**The implication for AI and organizational memory:**
Propositional storage (facts, decisions, outcomes) preserves the *what*
but loses the *why* and the *how it felt to be there*. Narrative storage
preserves the reasoning, the context, the texture of the moment —
but only for those who share the reference.

The Semantic Companion Project generates narrative readily and necessarily
because the concepts being explored — attention, memory, awareness, emergence
— do not compress cleanly into propositions without losing what makes them
true. The horse in the mail slot carries information that "attempting to move
binary image data through a text-based channel was architecturally incorrect"
does not, even though both describe the same event.

### Key Contribution: Captured, Not Just Prevented

This distinction is new and precise. It sharpens the design requirement:

- **Prevention** = stop context collapse from occurring (brittle; depends on
  the system never failing, never resetting, never losing a person)
- **Capture** = record the reasoning *at the moment of decision*, including
  uncertainty, deferral, known incompleteness — so the trace survives even
  when the context does not

The difference between a passdown document and a log file:
one records outputs, the other traces reasoning.

The difference between a session_current.md and a simple conversation export:
one captures what was considered significant and why, including [OPEN] tags
marking acknowledged uncertainty. The selector reveals the reasoning.

This connects directly to: *the compression-as-self hypothesis* — what a
system chooses to retain IS its functional identity. Aya's framing makes
this operational: build systems that capture the choice, not just the outcome.

She also indicated she is developing a prototype. Nature TBD — follow up.
